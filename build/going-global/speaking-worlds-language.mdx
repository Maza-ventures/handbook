Speaking the world's language isn't just about translating your product into English. It's about understanding the cultural, legal, and business norms that global companies expect. This includes everything from how you structure contracts to how you communicate with enterprise customers to how you handle data privacy.

The mistake most MENA startups make is trying to force global customers to adapt to local business practices. But the companies that succeed internationally are the ones that learn to operate according to global standards while maintaining their local advantages in terms of cost, talent, and market insight.
